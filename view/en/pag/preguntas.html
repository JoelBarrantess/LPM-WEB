Frequently asked questions
What does our NGO do?

Our NGO is dedicated to promoting computer education and benefits among underprivileged communities. We work to provide access to the education and computer skills necessary to thrive in the digital age.

How can I collaborate with the NGO?

There are several ways in which you can collaborate with our NGO. You can make a monetary donation, volunteer your time in our programs and workshops, or help spread the word about our work through social media and other platforms. All forms of support are welcome!

Where do we hold our programs and workshops?

We implement our programs and workshops in disadvantaged communities, local educational centers, libraries and others accessible to the participants. We work closely with communities to identify needs and deliver services where they are needed most.

Who can participate in our workshops?

Our workshops are open to people of all ages and backgrounds. We strive to reach students, adults, women, men, and anyone interested in learning about computing and improving their digital skills. No prior knowledge is required, as our workshops are designed to accommodate different levels of experience.

Do we offer certificates or diplomas at the end of the workshops?

Yes, we award certificates of participation to attendees who successfully complete our workshops. These certificates can serve as recognition of acquired skills and can be useful for future educational or employment opportunities.

How can we contact the NGO?

You can contact us through our website, where you will find a contact telephone number and an email address. We will be happy to answer your questions and provide you with more information about our activities.
